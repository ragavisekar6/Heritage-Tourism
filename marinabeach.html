Marina Beach

A Symphony of Colors – Where Flowers Bloom and Birds Sing!

Lose yourself in the vibrant beauty of meticulously crafted gardens.

Beach Highlights

Marina Beach, stretching over 13 kilometers along the Bay of Bengal, is not only the longest urban beach in India but also one of the most iconic landmarks of Chennai. Lined with historic memorials, statues, and colonial-era buildings, Marina is a bustling blend of sea breeze, local food stalls, cricket games, horse rides, and vibrant evening life. The early mornings and sunset hours see joggers, tourists, and photographers enjoying the scenic beauty. It is also home to important sites like the Anna and MGR Memorials, making it a cultural and political hub in the city.

Opening Hours

  • Open 24 hours (though best visited between 4:00 AM – 8:00 AM and 4:00 PM – 9:00 PM for safety and views)

Entry Fees:

  • No entry fee. Beach is public and open to all.

Transportation Options

  • Nearest Railway Station: Chennai Beach Station
  • Metro: Government Estate / Triplicane Metro Stations
  • Fare: Free if walking, ₹30-₹50 for auto.

Nearby Heritage Stays & Hotels

  • Taj Connemara
  • Hotel Marina Inn

Famous Food Places

  • Astoria Veg Restaurant - South Indian (Idli, Vada, Sambar)
  • The Silver Oak Restaurant - Tandoori & North Indian cuisine

Must-Try Specialties

  • Filter Coffee – Best at Saravana Bhavan or Madras Coffee House
  • Murugan Idli – Soft idlis and chutneys at Murugan Idli Shop
  • Chettinad Chicken – Best at Anjappar or Karaikudi Restaurant
  • Meen Kuzhambu (Fish Curry) – Best at Sea Shells or Maplai
  • Onion Rava Dosa – Crispy and golden at Saravana Bhavan
  • Chicken 65 – Originated in Chennai, try at Buhari Hotel
