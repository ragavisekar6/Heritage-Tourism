Kurunji Andavar Temple (Kodaikanal)

A Temple Beyond Time, A Devotion Beyond Life!

Generations have sought solace here, leaving behind echoes of unwavering faith.

Temple Highlights

Dedicated to Lord Murugan, the Kurinji Andavar Temple is one of Kodaikanal’s most spiritually significant landmarks. The temple derives its name from the Kurinji flower, which blooms once every twelve years in the Western Ghats. Situated on a hilltop, the temple offers a stunning view of the Palani Hills and the distant plains of Tamil Nadu. The serene ambiance and spiritual aura make it a peaceful retreat for devotees and tourists alike. The temple is built in traditional Tamil architecture, featuring intricate carvings and a beautifully decorated sanctum. Pilgrims visit the temple to seek blessings from Lord Murugan, the Hindu god of war and victory. The temple premises are often filled with the soothing sounds of devotional hymns and the fragrance of fresh flowers and incense. One of the highlights of the temple is its annual festival, which attracts thousands of devotees from across the region. Apart from its religious significance, the temple is a fantastic place for nature lovers, as the surrounding landscape is adorned with lush greenery and exotic flora. The best time to visit is during sunrise or sunset when the temple and its surroundings are bathed in golden hues, creating a divine experience.

Festive Seasons

  • Kurinji Flower Festival (when the Kurinji blooms every 12 years – next bloom expected around 2030).
  • Summer Festival (May – cultural programs, processions).
  • Chitra Pournami (April–May – full moon celebration).
