Kurangani Hills

A Silent Tribute to the Brave, Where Every Brick Holds a Story!

Step into the heart of remembrance, where courage and sacrifice are immortalized, and the spirit of the past whispers through the ages.

Hill Station Highlights

Kurangani Hills is a picturesque hill station known for its dense forests, cool climate, and trekking trails leading up to Top Station in Munnar. Popular with adventure seekers, Kurangani offers breathtaking valley views, streams, and unique flora and fauna.

Opening Hours

  • It is advisible to visit during daytime.

Best Time to Visit

  • November to February – Ideal for trekking and camping.

Entry Fees:

  • Trekking permit fee of ₹200 (collected at the forest check-post).

Transportation Options

  • Nearest Town: Bodinayakanur (16 km away), taxis and jeeps available
  • Nearest railway station: Madurai Junction (95 km away)

Nearby Heritage Stays & Hotels

  • Kurangani Farm Stay
  • Green Hills Cottages, Bodinayakanur

Famous Food Places

  • Local mess at Bodinayakanur – Traditional Tamil meals
  • Tea and snacks along trekking routes

Must-Try Specialties

  • Theni Mutton Biryani – Hotel Sri Gowri Shankar (Near Theni Bus Stand).
  • Seeraga Samba Biryani – Venu Biriyani Hotel (Periyakulam Road, Theni).
  • Ayira Meen Kuzhambu (Small Fish Curry) – Local Messes in Vaigai Dam area.
  • Banana Chips – Made from local Nendran bananas, available in street shops near Theni Market.
  • Karupatti Coffee – Traditional palm jaggery coffee from roadside tea stalls in Cumbum & Uthamapalayam.
  • Palani Panchamirtham – Available in shops near temples and local sweet shops across Theni.
  • Elaneer Payasam – Served in select restaurants near Lower Camp & Bodi Mettu.
