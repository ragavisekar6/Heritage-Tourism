Kapaleeshwarar Temple, Mylapore

A Divine Legacy – Carved in Stone, Etched in Faith!

Wander through halls of devotion, where deities watch over generations with unwavering grace and strength.

Temple Highlights

Kapaleeshwarar Temple, located in the heart of Mylapore, is one of the oldest and most revered temples in Chennai. Dedicated to Lord Shiva in the form of Kapaleeshwarar and his consort Goddess Karpagambal, the temple is a fine example of Dravidian architecture with its towering gopurams adorned with intricate sculptures. Originally believed to have been built by the Pallavas in the 7th century and later rebuilt by the Vijayanagara rulers, the temple is surrounded by a lively bazaar and holds immense cultural and spiritual significance. The annual Panguni festival draws thousands of devotees and tourists alike.

Opening Hours

  • 5:00 AM - 12:00 PM
  • 4:00 PM - 9:00 PM

Festive Seasons

Panguni Peruvizha (March–April), featuring the Arupathimoovar festival.

Darshan and Entry Fees

  • Free entry; special poojas may have nominal charges.

Transportation Options

  • Accessible via Mylapore MRTS station; ample bus and auto services.
