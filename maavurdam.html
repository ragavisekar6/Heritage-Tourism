Maavur Lake Dam (Sirumalai)

Unleash Your Soul Amidst Nature’s Unspoiled Wonders!

Breathe in the fresh air, listen to the whispers of the wind, and let the scenic beauty transport you to a world of peace and tranquility.

Dam Highlights

Maavur Lake Dam is a picturesque and significant structure located in the Sirumalai hills of Tamil Nadu. Nestled amidst the lush greenery of the Western Ghats, this dam is built across the Maavur Lake and serves as a vital water source for the surrounding region, particularly for the irrigation of nearby agricultural lands. The dam is also a key component of the region's water management system, contributing to the development of agriculture, including the cultivation of crops like tea, vegetables, and fruits that thrive in the cool climate of Sirumalai.Historically, the construction of the Maavur Lake Dam can be traced back to the early 20th century when it was built to address the growing needs of water conservation and irrigation. Over time, it has become an integral part of the local economy, supporting farming activities and providing water for local communities.Apart from its functional purpose, the Maavur Lake Dam is also a serene and scenic spot, offering visitors a chance to enjoy the beauty of the surrounding landscape. The dam area is often surrounded by dense forests and rolling hills, making it a great location for nature walks and photography. The peaceful environment and the cool climate around the dam make it a popular spot for tourists seeking tranquility and a break from the hustle and bustle of city life.The dam's proximity to other tourist destinations in Sirumalai also adds to its appeal, as visitors can combine a trip to the dam with a visit to the nearby viewpoints and temples. Whether you're an avid nature lover, a photography enthusiast, or someone simply looking to relax and enjoy the serene beauty of the region, Maavur Lake Dam offers a peaceful retreat in the heart of Sirumalai's scenic hills.

Opening Hours

  • 9:00 AM - 6:00 PM

Best Time to Visit

  • August to December, when the dam is full, and the surrounding gardens are in bloom.

Entry Fees:

  • No Entry Fee.

Transportation Options

  • From Dindigul Bus Stand (30 km): Best reached via car or hired jeep through forest trails. No direct bus route. Taxi fare: ₹1,000–₹1,200.
  • Dindigul Junction (29 km) – Taxi fare: ₹900–₹1,100.
  • Madurai Airport (100 km) – Taxi fare: ₹2,000–₹2,300.

Nearby Heritage Stays & Hotels
