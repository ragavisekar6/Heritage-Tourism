La Saleth Church (Kodaikanal)

Bathed in the Golden Glow of Candlelight and Prayer!

Experience the warmth of unwavering belief under vaulted ceilings.

Church Highlights

La Saleth Church is a 150-year-old Roman Catholic shrine that blends Tamil and Gothic architectural styles. It is one of the oldest churches in Kodaikanal, built in 1866 by French missionaries and dedicated to Our Lady of La Salette. The church is perched atop a hill, offering breathtaking views of the surrounding landscapes, making it not only a place of worship but also a popular tourist attraction. The twin spires, vibrant stained-glass windows, and intricate wooden work inside reflect its historical and artistic significance. The annual festival celebrated in August attracts thousands of devotees who gather here for prayers and special processions. During this time, the church is beautifully decorated, and the atmosphere is filled with devotion and festivity. The peaceful ambiance of La Saleth Church makes it an ideal spot for meditation and reflection. Whether you're a history enthusiast, an architecture lover, or a traveler seeking spiritual solace, visiting this iconic church in Kodaikanal is a rewarding experience.
