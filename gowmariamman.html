Sri Gowmariamman Temple

Myth, Majesty & Miracles – A Temple’s Timeless Embrace!

Every stone whispers legends of gods, saints, and devotees who shaped the soul of this divine space.

Temple Highlights

Sri Gowmariamman Temple in Veerapandi is one of the most revered temples in Theni district, dedicated to Goddess Gowmariamman, the embodiment of strength and protection. The temple is especially famous for its grand Chithirai festival, celebrated with vibrant processions, traditional music, and rituals that attract thousands of devotees from across Tamil Nadu and Kerala. Located on the banks of the river Vaigai, the temple offers a serene and divine atmosphere, making it both a spiritual and cultural landmark.

Opening Hours

6:00 AM – 1:00 PM, 4:00 PM – 8:30 PM

Festive Seasons

  • Chithirai Festival (April-May) – Grand 8-day celebration with processions, rituals, and cultural performances
  • Aadi Month Fridays (July-August) – Special Pujas and crowd gatherings

Entry Fees

  • Free

Transportation Options

  • Theni Bus Stand (6 km away) – Auto fare approx. ₹80-₹120
  • Nearest Railway Station: Dindigul Junction (75 km away), cabs and buses available
  • Local Buses:
    1. Route 1: Theni → Veerapandi (every 20 minutes)
    2. Route 2: Periyakulam → Veerapandi (frequent service)
