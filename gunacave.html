Guna Cave (Devil's Kitchen)

Echoes of the Earth – Where Shadows Tell Ancient Stories!

Step into the mysterious depths of time, where every rock formation whispers secrets of a forgotten past.

Cave Highlights

Hidden amidst dense forests, Guna Caves, also known as Devil’s Kitchen, is one of the most mysterious and thrilling attractions in Kodaikanal. The caves earned their name after the famous Tamil movie "Guna" was filmed here. Located deep within the Shola forests, these naturally formed caves are believed to be over a thousand years old and are associated with local folklore. Legends suggest that ancient sages used the caves for meditation, and some myths claim that the caves are bottomless. The eerie atmosphere, combined with massive rock formations and thick tree roots hanging around the entrance, adds to the enigmatic charm of the caves. The pathway leading to the caves is an adventure in itself, with tall trees and a misty ambiance creating a sense of mystery. However, due to safety concerns, direct access to the caves is restricted, but visitors can still view them from a designated viewpoint. Guna Caves is a must-visit for thrill-seekers and nature enthusiasts looking to explore Kodaikanal's lesser-known yet fascinating spots. The best time to visit is during the early morning when the mist adds a surreal touch to the entire landscape.

Opening Hours

9:00 AM - 4:30 PM

Best time to visit:

Monsoon Season (June–September): Best time for a thrilling cave exploration with a mystical atmosphere.
