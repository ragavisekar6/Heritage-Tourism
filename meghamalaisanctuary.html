Megamalai Wildlife Sanctuary

A Silent Tribute to the Brave, Where Every Brick Holds a Story!

Step into the heart of remembrance, where courage and sacrifice are immortalized, and the spirit of the past whispers through the ages.

Sanctuary Highlights

Megamalai Wildlife Sanctuary is part of the Western Ghats and known for its thick forests, tea plantations, mountain streams, and endemic species. Wildlife sightings include elephants, gaurs, leopards, Indian giant squirrels, and a wide range of butterflies and birds. The lush Meghamalai hills are also ideal for treks, waterfalls, and scenic viewpoints.

Opening Hours

  • 6:00 AM – 5:00 PM.

Best Time to Visit

  • November to April.

Entry Fees:

  • Indian Tourists: ₹60 per person.
  • Guide and vehicle entry charges extra.

Activities Available

  • Wildlife photography
  • Trekking trails in Meghamalai
  • Visiting Manalaru Dam and waterfalls

Transportation Options

  • Theni Town (70 km away)
  • Nearest railway station: Madurai Junction (130 km)
  • Private taxis required for hill access

Nearby Heritage Stays & Hotels

  • Forest Rest Houses in Meghamalai – Pre-booking needed
  • Tea Estate Guest Houses

Famous Food Places

  • Local messes in Chinnamanur – vegetarian and non-vegetarian meals
  • Tea and fresh spice products available in Meghamalai

Must-Try Specialties

  • Theni Mutton Biryani – Hotel Sri Gowri Shankar (Near Theni Bus Stand).
  • Seeraga Samba Biryani – Venu Biriyani Hotel (Periyakulam Road, Theni).
  • Ayira Meen Kuzhambu (Small Fish Curry) – Local Messes in Vaigai Dam area.
  • Banana Chips – Made from local Nendran bananas, available in street shops near Theni Market.
  • Karupatti Coffee – Traditional palm jaggery coffee from roadside tea stalls in Cumbum & Uthamapalayam.
  • Palani Panchamirtham – Available in shops near temples and local sweet shops across Theni.
  • Elaneer Payasam – Served in select restaurants near Lower Camp & Bodi Mettu.
