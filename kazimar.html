Mosque Highlights

Kazimar Periya Pallivasal, the oldest mosque in Madurai, is a living testament to Islamic heritage in South India. Built in the 13th century by Hazrat Kazi Syed Tajuddin, a direct descendant of Prophet Muhammad, the mosque has remained under the care of his lineage for over 700 years. With its ancient domes, intricate minbar carvings, and a peaceful prayer hall, the mosque blends Tamil and Indo-Islamic architecture. The mosque is not just a place of worship but a center of Islamic learning, housing a madrasa (religious school) where Arabic and Islamic teachings are imparted. The tomb of the founding Sufi saint, located within the mosque premises, attracts devotees seeking blessings. Beyond its spiritual significance, Kazimar Big Mosque is a symbol of harmony, representing the cultural synthesis that defines Madurai‚Äôs diverse religious landscape.
