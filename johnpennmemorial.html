Colonel John Pennycuick Memorial

A Silent Tribute to the Brave, Where Every Brick Holds a Story!

Step into the heart of remembrance, where courage and sacrifice are immortalized, and the spirit of the past whispers through the ages.

Memorial Highlights

The Colonel John Pennycuick Memorial is located in Lower Camp, approximately 8 kilometers from Gudalur in Tamil Nadu's Theni District. This memorial honors Colonel John Pennycuick, the British engineer renowned for constructing the Mullaperiyar Dam in 1895, a project that significantly transformed irrigation and agriculture in the region.The memorial features a 7.5-foot bronze statue of Pennycuick, weighing about 575 kilograms, symbolizing his enduring legacy. Inside, visitors can explore black-and-white photographs depicting various stages of the dam's construction, Pennycuick's residence during that period, and the living quarters of the workers involved. A replica of the Mullaperiyar Dam is also displayed, providing deeper insights into this monumental engineering feat.Entry to the memorial is free, and it is easily accessible by road, with ample parking available. The nearest railway station is Dindigul Junction, approximately 65 kilometers away, while the closest airport is Madurai International Airport.For the local farming community, the memorial holds profound significance, often regarded as a temple honoring Pennycuick's contributions to their livelihoods. His dedication to the Mullaperiyar Dam project, including personal sacrifices, has left an indelible mark on the region's history and development.

Opening Hours

Transportation Options

  • By Air: The nearest airport is Madurai Airport, approximately 125 kilometers away.
  • By Train: The closest major railway station is Madurai Junction, about 125 kilometers from the memorial.
  • By Road: The memorial is located 8 kilometers from Gudalur in Theni District. Regular bus services and taxis are available from nearby towns and cities.

Entry Fees:

  • Free.
