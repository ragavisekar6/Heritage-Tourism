Thiru Avinankudi Temple (Palani)

Carved in Stone, Enshrined in Faith

Witness the mesmerizing fusion of mythology and craftsmanship, where every pillar echoes the whispers of saints and sages.

Temple Highlights

Located at the foothills of Palani Murugan Temple, Thiru Avinankudi is one of the oldest temples dedicated to Lord Murugan and is considered the third of his six abodes (Arupadai Veedu). This temple holds immense spiritual energy and is believed to be where Lord Murugan grew up as a child before ascending to the hilltop.Unlike the hill temple, which represents Murugan in his ascetic form, Thiru Avinankudi houses him as a benevolent deity with Goddess Valli and Deivanai by his side. The temple is beautifully decorated, with a grand entrance, intricately carved pillars, and a peaceful temple pond.One of the most intriguing aspects of this temple is the presence of the Navagraha shrine, which is not commonly found in Murugan temples. Devotees believe that praying at Thiru Avinankudi before ascending the hill temple ensures a smooth and blessed pilgrimage.The temple is famous for Vaikasi Visakam and Thai Poosam, when devotees undertake long processions and special rituals. Surrounded by lush greenery and serene landscapes, Thiru Avinankudi is a spiritual haven that offers both divine blessings and a tranquil retreat.
